01 — Fundamentals

What is a Stock Chart?

A stock chart is a visual graph that shows how a stock's price has changed over time. Just like a weather chart shows temperature over days, a stock chart shows price over time.

Key Idea: The X-axis (horizontal) always represents time. The Y-axis (vertical) always represents price. Every chart type is just a different way to display this same relationship.
📅

Time Periods

Charts can show prices by minute, hour, day, week, or month. Day traders look at minutes; long-term investors look at months or years.

💰

Price

The price plotted is usually the closing price — the last price a stock traded at before the market closed for the day.

📊

Volume

Volume is the number of shares traded. High volume = more people buying/selling. Often shown as bars at the bottom of a chart.

📈

Trend

A trend is the general direction of a price. Uptrend = prices going up. Downtrend = prices going down.

03 — Candlestick Charts

The Candlestick Chart

The most popular chart among traders. Each "candle" shows 4 prices for a single day: Open, High, Low, and Close (OHLC). Hover over candles to see these values!

Green Candle (Bullish) 🐂

The closing price was higher than the opening price. The price went UP that day.

Red Candle (Bearish) 🐻

The closing price was lower than the opening price. The price went DOWN that day.

The Wick (Shadow): The thin lines above and below the body are called wicks. The top wick = day's highest price. Bottom wick = day's lowest price.

06 — Quick Reference

Glossary of Terms

A cheat-sheet of essential stock chart vocabulary.

Bull Market

A market where prices are rising or expected to rise. Investors are optimistic. 🐂

Bear Market

A market where prices are falling (20%+ drop from recent highs). Investors are pessimistic. 🐻

OHLC

Open, High, Low, Close — the four key price points shown on a candlestick chart each day.

Support Level

A price floor where a stock tends to stop falling and bounce back up. Buyers step in here.

Resistance Level

A price ceiling where a stock tends to stop rising and fall back. Sellers step in here.

Moving Average

The average price of a stock over a set number of days. Smooths out short-term price noise.

Volume

Number of shares traded in a period. High volume confirms the strength of a price move.

RSI

Relative Strength Index. Measures if a stock is overbought (above 70) or oversold (below 30).

Wick / Shadow

The thin lines on a candlestick showing the highest and lowest prices reached during that period.

Trend

The general direction prices are moving — uptrend (higher highs) or downtrend (lower lows).

Breakout

When price moves above a resistance level or below a support level, often with high volume.

Consolidation

A period where price moves sideways with no clear trend. The market is "taking a rest."
